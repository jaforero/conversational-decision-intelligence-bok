# Initial controlled glossary

!!! info "How to read this glossary"
    From `v0.4.0`, this is the initial controlled CDI-BoK vocabulary. PULSE-owned concepts reproduce or summarize its DNA and do not acquire an independent definition here.

| Term | Controlled definition | Conceptual owner | Key limit |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Action** | Execution of a decision through an observable change in behavior, allocation, workflow, rule or control. | PULSE DNA | An unexecuted recommendation is not action. |
| **Conversational Analytics** | Governed natural-language interaction with organizational evidence to ask, explain, explore and follow up. | PULSE DNA | Does not imply decision, truth or authorization. |
| **Learning** | Adjustment from comparing expectation with result and changing perception, assumptions, criteria, decisions, actions or controls. | PULSE DNA | Recording a lesson without changing the next cycle is insufficient. |
| **CDI-BoK** | Public, governed and versioned system organizing official CDI project knowledge. | CDI-GOV-001 | Not automatically a recognized standard. |
| **Context** | Processes, rules, history, constraints, exceptions, objectives, relations and local information that give evidence operational meaning. | PULSE DNA | More documents do not guarantee sufficient context. |
| **Decision conversation** | Iterative Human–AI interaction that clarifies evidence, assumptions, alternatives, criteria and next steps for an explicit decision. | CDI Constitution | Not casual conversation or simple NLQ. |
| **Conversational Decision Intelligence (CDI)** | Proposed interdisciplinary domain studying and designing how humans and AI collaborate through conversations to turn evidence and context into decision, action and learning. | CDI Constitution | Official project definition; external recognition pending. |
| **Decision copilot** | AI-enabled system supporting a person with evidence, context, options, scenarios and workflow without assuming final accountability. | PULSE DNA | Copilot does not mean automatic approval. |
| **Decision criterion** | Attribute or rule for comparing alternatives against objectives, risks and constraints. | CDI foundational core | Must not be confused with a performance metric. |
| **Trusted data** | Evidence with quality, provenance, definition, ownership, security and relevance sufficient for a specific use. | PULSE DNA | Always fitness for purpose, not absolute perfection. |
| **Decision** | Committed choice among alternatives by an authorized owner under constraints and uncertainty. | PULSE DNA | An opinion or uncommitted recommendation is not a decision. |
| **Decision Circle** | PULSE cycle: Perceive, Focus, Anticipate, Generate options, Decide and act, Learn. | PULSE DNA | Not a rigid waterfall. |
| **Decision Experience** | Complete human and operational experience through which evidence is perceived, meaning understood, options evaluated, a decision made, action taken and learning produced. | PULSE DNA | Not a synonym for visual interface. |
| **Decision Intelligence** | Coordinated use of evidence, analysis, human judgment, technology, governance and learning to improve a decision system. | PULSE DNA | External definitions vary. |
| **Decision latency** | Time between availability of a relevant signal and a sufficiently informed, authorized decision. | PULSE DNA | Lower is not always better if legitimacy or safety falls. |
| **Evidence** | Recorded or observable material capable of supporting or challenging a claim. | PULSE DNA | Not automatically complete, certain or causal. |
| **Human-in-Control** | Design and governance condition in which humans retain authority over purpose, criteria, limits, risk, accountability, override and consequences. | PULSE DNA | Participation in a loop does not prove effective control. |
| **Uncertainty** | Relevant lack of certainty about data, models, explanations, scenarios, actions or results. | CDI foundational core | Must inform the decision, not serve as a generic disclaimer. |
| **Idea** | Business meaning derived from understanding: explanation, opportunity, risk or possible direction. | PULSE DNA | An insight is not yet a decision. |
| **Organizational Intelligence** | Strengthened collective capacity to understand, decide, act, learn and anticipate across repeated cycles. | PULSE DNA | A capability, not a technology score. |
| **Decision owner** | Person or body with legitimate authority and accountability over a defined decision. | CDI foundational core | Facilitating, recommending or executing does not imply ownership. |
| **PDAMR** | PULSE compass: Priority, Decision, Action, Metric, Risk. | PULSE DNA | Orients an initiative; not a complete project plan. |
| **PULSE** | Human-centered Decision Intelligence methodology and operating philosophy reducing distance between organizational reality and better decisions through trusted data, context, understanding, action and learning. | PULSE DNA / Identity | The complete official English definition remains canonical. |
| **Recommendation** | Proposed course of action with reasons, evidence, assumptions, constraints and risk. | PULSE DNA | Not a decision until an authorized owner commits. |
| **Result** | Observable consequence of action, including intended and unintended effects. | PULSE DNA | A favorable result proves neither a good decision nor causality. |
| **Decision system** | People, rights, evidence, process, models, interfaces, actions, controls and feedback producing repeated decisions. | CDI foundational core | Not necessarily software. |
| **Traceability** | Ability to reconstruct definitions, sources, transformations, assumptions, versions, recommendations, decisions and actions. | CDI-GOV-001 / PULSE DNA | A technical log without business meaning is insufficient. |

## Usage rules

1. A controlled definition has one conceptual owner.
2. Derived pages may summarize it but must link to or state the owner.
3. English terms remain where they are canonical PULSE language or prevent ambiguity.
4. **Must**, **should**, **may** and **must not** are reserved for requirements, recommendations, options and prohibitions.
5. A semantic change requires impact analysis across navigation, claims, sources, cases and retrieval systems.
