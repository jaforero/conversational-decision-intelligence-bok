# Conversational decision patterns

<div class="cdi-insight" markdown>
**A pattern does not decide for you.** It structures a recurring conversation so the decision, evidence, rights, action and learning no longer remain implicit.
</div>

This area activates **Decision Patterns** and **Decision Anti-patterns** as a candidate CDI-BoK taxonomy. It does not claim five patterns cover every decision or improve results. It exposes minimum reasoning and helps teams select the least complex structure for the current blocker.

## Problem addressed

| Moment | Control question | Candidate pattern |
|---|---|---|
| Frame | What decision and priority are at stake? | **Decision contract** |
| Ground | What do we know, from where, and what is uncertain? | **Evidence boundary** |
| Deliberate | What material alternatives survive challenge? | **Option challenge** |
| Commit | Who decides and what governed action follows? | **Decision–action handoff** |
| Learn | What changed between expectation and result? | **Expectation–result review** |

This is not a mandatory sequence. Skip already-satisfied moves. Missing material evidence or authority may correctly end in **abstention or escalation**.

## Quick selection

| Observable signal | Start with | Stop if |
|---|---|---|
| “Analyze” has no choice | Decision contract | No owner, alternative or deadline emerges |
| Same metric has different meanings | Evidence boundary | Definition or permission cannot be resolved |
| Only one preferred option exists | Option challenge | Alternatives would be artificial or an emergency rule applies |
| Recommendation exists but no action follows | Decision–action handoff | The participant lacks resource authority |
| Horizon ended and expectations differ | Expectation–result review | No prior snapshot; only exploratory review is possible |

### Match rigor to risk

Use the minimum move for low-risk reversible choices; add sources, alternatives, guardrails and explicit approval for medium risk; require expert review, stronger evidence, permission, traceability and a non-automated path for high risk, low reversibility or rights impact.

## Two catalogs, one discipline

The [pattern catalog](catalog.md) describes structures that may advance a decision. The [anti-pattern catalog](anti-patterns.md) describes recurring behaviors that impair traceability, control or learning. The [language and template](pattern-language.md) governs new entries. The [B2B walkthrough](b2b-proposal-walkthrough.md) demonstrates that a protocol must stop before inventing an owner or outcome.

## Authority boundaries

This candidate derives from PULSE without redefining it; implements an institutional taxonomy, not an academic standard; separates reuse, conformance and effectiveness; does not require a conversational interface; and grants no AI system implicit decision or execution rights.
