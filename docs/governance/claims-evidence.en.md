# Claims and evidence

## Two independent dimensions

A claim may have high institutional authority and low empirical strength, or strong external evidence without yet being CDI-BoK doctrine.

| Dimension | Question |
|---|---|
| **Authority** | Who may define or change this wording within the project? |
| **Evidence** | How well is the claim supported beyond project authority? |

## Minimum classes

- **Project definition:** establishes official internal language.
- **Empirical:** describes a testable observation.
- **Causal:** states that an intervention produces a result.
- **Comparative:** claims superiority or difference from an alternative.
- **Forecast:** anticipates a future state under assumptions.
- **Aspiration:** states a desired direction.
- **Original contribution proposal:** proposes novelty pending adequate comparison.

## Originality rule

Authorship does not prove international novelty. An original contribution requires a problem, comparison with prior work, proposed difference, limits, validation status and evolution path. Without sufficient comparative review it must retain the label **Original Contribution Proposal**.
