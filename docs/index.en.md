## What you will be able to do

The CDI-BoK turns interdisciplinary knowledge into practical capability. Every path ends with an artifact or criterion you can apply to a decision.

<div class="cdi-outcomes">
<div class="cdi-outcome"><span>01</span><h3>Frame</h3><p>Define the decision, owner, alternatives, criteria, constraints and cost of waiting or being wrong.</p></div>
<div class="cdi-outcome"><span>02</span><h3>Evaluate</h3><p>Separate facts, calculations, inferences and hypotheses; expose quality, context and uncertainty.</p></div>
<div class="cdi-outcome"><span>03</span><h3>Design</h3><p>Select the interface and information sequence that best support understanding, deciding and acting.</p></div>
<div class="cdi-outcome"><span>04</span><h3>Govern</h3><p>Assign decision rights, permissions, boundaries, escalation and human control over AI.</p></div>
<div class="cdi-outcome"><span>05</span><h3>Learn</h3><p>Compare expectation with outcome, separate decision quality from luck and improve the next cycle.</p></div>
</div>

## An architecture for decisions, not another technology ladder

<div class="cdi-flow" aria-label="Decision value chain">
<span>Trusted data</span><span>Context</span><span>Understanding</span><span>Options</span><span>Decision</span><span>Action</span><span>Result</span><span>Learning</span>

Technology can support the entire chain but cannot replace purpose, judgment, authority or accountability. **PULSE** provides the framework of practice; **CDI** studies and designs conversational collaboration; the **CDI-BoK** organizes knowledge, sources and limits.

    CDI is a **proposed interdisciplinary domain of practice**, and PULSE is the project's methodology and operating philosophy. The portal seeks useful, testable knowledge; it does not claim academic recognition, priority over the term or generalizable effectiveness.

## Practice shows the method; it does not invent outcomes

The Practice Lab contains demonstrations and instrumentation. The current B2B case uses simulated data and remains **instrumented, not executed**. It shows how a decision is framed, a weak claim is blocked and a protocol is prepared; it does not demonstrate organizational impact.
