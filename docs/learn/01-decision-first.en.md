# 1. Frame the decision

**Module outcome:** a decision sentence and sufficiently complete PDAMR compass to know what evidence to seek and what action could change.

## The question before technology

PULSE starts with: **What decision do we want to improve?** A priority is not a decision; neither is a problem. An insight or recommendation still does not commit a choice.

| Statement | What it is | What is missing |
|---|---|---|
| “Sales are falling” | Signal or problem | Who must choose what to do and when |
| “Why is conversion falling?” | Diagnostic question | The choice that will use the answer |
| “We should train the team” | Recommendation | Alternatives, criteria and authority to commit resources |
| “By Friday, the sales director will choose among redesigning the proposal, changing follow-up or not intervening” | Framed decision | Evidence, criteria, constraints, action and learning |

## The decision sentence

> **By [date], [owner] must choose [one option] among [alternatives] to move [priority], using [criteria], under [constraints]. The choice will change [action].**

A good sentence exposes timing, real owner, alternatives including no action where legitimate, priority, criteria, resource/regulatory/capacity/risk constraints, and subsequent action.

## PDAMR compass

| Component | Question | Evidence of a useful answer |
|---|---|---|
| **Priority** | What material priority must move? | Baseline, horizon and consequence |
| **Decision** | What choice must improve? | Owner, options and date |
| **Action** | What will be done differently? | Observable behavioral, process or allocation change |
| **Metric** | How will we know? | Outcome, process and guardrails |
| **Risk** | What can go wrong and who controls it? | Harm, permissions, escalation and stop conditions |

PDAMR tests **minimum completeness**, not quality. A complete frame can still contain bad premises.

## Hidden costs

- **Wrong-action cost:** waste, harm, inequity or lost trust.
- **Delay cost:** lost opportunity, accumulation or de-facto choice through inaction.
- **Reversal cost:** difficulty of undoing the option.
- **Information cost:** time and resources to reduce uncertainty.

“Research more” is superior only when expected information value exceeds cost and arrives before the decision loses usefulness.

## Stress test

- Can the owner truly commit the action?
- Are options materially different?
- What would we prefer without new data?
- What information could change that preference?
- Who bears consequences without participating?
- If irreversible, what extra evidence is required?
