# Candidate v0.7.0-rc.1

`v0.7.0-rc.1` starts Sprint 5—**Decision Quality & Measurement**. It closes a declared gap: the portal explained why to measure and learn, but lacked a precise system for decision metrics and separation of expected from observed results.

## What it adds

- Decision Quality guidance separating ex-ante process from outcome;
- a seven-dimension profile, evidence and blockers;
- a six-lens Decision Measurement System;
- contracts, formulas and limits for latency, execution fidelity, learning closure and calibration;
- Human–AI collaboration criteria;
- anti-metrics preventing adoption, speed, override or isolated ROI from standing in for value;
- a YAML Decision Measurement Record with before/after snapshots;
- ADR-021, sources, claims and reproducible gates.

## Critical design decision

There is no universal **Decision Health Score**. One score would ease benchmarking but could hide critical blockers, mix incomparable decisions and invite gaming. The release keeps a multidimensional profile until real evidence supports weights or benchmarks.

## Unchanged boundaries

The foundational core retains authority; the B2B case remains unexecuted; the learning experience remains candidate guidance; instrumentation is not claimed to improve outcomes; the system is not a validated scientific or maturity instrument.
