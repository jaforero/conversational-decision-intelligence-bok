# Candidate v0.8.0-rc.1

`v0.8.0-rc.1` starts Sprint 6—**Conversational Decision Patterns & Anti-patterns**. It turns two undeveloped domains into a candidate system for recognizing recurring blockers and selecting the smallest conversational move needed to frame, ground, deliberate, commit or learn.

## What it adds

- controlled language and anatomy for patterns and anti-patterns;
- five candidate patterns from decision contract to expectation–result review;
- six anti-patterns covering decorative conversation, semantic drift, artificial certainty, undue authority, adoption-as-impact and hindsight;
- proportional selection by blocker, stakes, uncertainty and reversibility;
- stop conditions, abstention, escalation and non-conversational alternatives;
- YAML template, null states and machine-readable registry;
- a stopped application to the simulated B2B case;
- ADR-023, calibrated institutional claims and a Sprint 6 gate.

## Critical design decision

The catalog is **neither a universal sequence nor a prompt library**. A pattern may run in a meeting, brief, workflow or governed assistant. When a rule, report or checklist works better, conversation should be omitted.

## Unchanged boundaries

The `v0.4.0` core retains authority; PULSE remains interface-independent; the future PULSE pattern documents are not replaced; the B2B case remains `instrumented-not-executed`; effectiveness, exhaustiveness, scientific validation and external recognition are not claimed.
