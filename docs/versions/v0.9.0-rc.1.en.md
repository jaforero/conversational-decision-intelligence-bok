# Candidate v0.9.0-rc.1

`v0.9.0-rc.1` turns the additional corpus into a traceable evidence backbone.
It separates established foundations, mature practices, emerging evidence,
PULSE synthesis, hypotheses and future signals.

## What it adds

- an audit of 12 sources with hash, provenance, completeness, rights, use and
  boundaries;
- evidence profiles across eight dimensions without an aggregate score;
- automated compatibility rules between source class and claim class;
- primary and official anchors for material claims;
- a DI state of the art, PULSE evidence map, future signals and research agenda
  in Spanish and English;
- version and `as_of` date for changing standards and frameworks.

## What remains blocked

- using incomplete copies to support claims;
- using AI-assisted syntheses as direct evidence;
- presenting forecasts as facts, causality or readiness;
- presenting candidate architecture or metaphors as validated doctrine;
- claiming CDI or PULSE effectiveness, superiority or scientific recognition;
- redistributing rights-reserved analyst PDF files.

## Status and authority

This is an untagged, ratified, merged and deployed release candidate. It does
not promote the approved `v0.4.0` core, learning, measurement, patterns or the
candidate PULSE architecture. Ratification confirms that the integrated
candidate is a valid base for continued work; it does not make it a stable
`v0.9.0` release or raise the strength of its claims.

The central thesis is provisional: DI is assessed as an emerging integrative
field built on mature disciplines rather than a consolidated scientific
discipline. A systematic review or contradictory evidence may change that
conclusion.
